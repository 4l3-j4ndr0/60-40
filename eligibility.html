ELIGIBILITY

This program is presented for state evaluation and authorization and does not constitute a lending offer.

Primary Beneficiaries

U.S. Military Veterans

  • Honorable or General discharge
  • Primary residence requirement
  • Affordability pressure, hardship, or foreclosure risk

Secondary Beneficiaries

First Responders (Florida)

  • Law enforcement
  • Firefighters
  • EMTs
  • Corrections officers
  • Active or retired Florida residents

Additional Eligible Participants

Hospital and healthcare employees

Hospital and healthcare employees affected by inflationary housing pressures (including eligible workers supporting veteran care systems, where applicable).

PRIMARY RESIDENCE REQUIREMENT

(STRICT)

Primary Residence Requirement

All program homes must be the participant’s primary residence.

This Rule Applies To

  • Original participants
  • Qualified heirs
  • Qualified subsequent buyers
  • Qualified family transferees

The program does not apply to:

  • Second homes
  • Vacation homes
  • Seasonal residences
  • Rental/investment properties
Important: Occupancy status is verified and enforced under program compliance standards.

SALES, TRANSFERS & TITLE RULES

Priority Order for Sale

If an original participant sells:

  1. Qualified Veteran
  2. Qualified First Responder
  3. Open market only if the buyer assumes both Note A and Note B and meets program requirements

Mandatory Assumption

Any buyer/heir/transferee seeking program benefits must:

  • Qualify under eligibility rules
  • Assume both Note A and Note B
  • Comply with primary residence and integrity rules

If both notes are not assumed, then:

  • Title cannot be transferred
  • Possession/keys cannot be delivered
  • Program benefits do not apply

Title Event Settlement Rule

At any title-related event (sale/refinance/transfer outside program rules), the participant must settle what is owed on both Note A and Note B.

FAMILY SUPPORT & CAREGIVER TRANSFERS

When a Veteran Needs Help at Home

If an original veteran homeowner needs a child or close family member to move in for caregiving support, the situation is reviewed for integrity and alignment with the veteran’s best interest.

Program Review

  • Compliance/fraud team verifies legitimacy
  • Provides guidance to reduce probate complications

This Rule Applies To

  • Original participants
  • Qualified heirs
  • Qualified subsequent buyers
  • Qualified family transferees
Important: Occupancy status is verified and enforced under program compliance standards.

FRAUD PREVENTION & PROGRAM INTEGRITY

The primary objective of this initiative is to formally and institutionally present a request to the appropriate authorities for the evaluation and potential implementation of a pilot program focused on stability and dignified access to housing solutions for veterans.

The project is initially developed at the state level, with the intention of serving as a replicable model in other jurisdictions, always within the applicable legal framework and with strict adherence to the privacy of participants.

The program includes safeguards to prevent abuse and speculation, including:

  • Primary residence verification
  • Anti-flipping enforcement
  • Mandatory assumption of both notes
  • Compliance review of caregiver transfers
  • Documented eligibility confirmation

The program is designed to protect participants and the State—not to enable short-term profit-taking.
